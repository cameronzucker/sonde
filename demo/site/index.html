SONDE HF DATA MODEM
ARDOP · SIMULATED CHANNEL EMCOMM · SITREP DELIVERY
▲

Two real modems · a live connection · simulated channel · not on-air. Each session stands up two real ARDOP stations (ardopcf) and runs an actual connection between them — CONNECT handshake, bandwidth negotiation, and adaptive-rate ARQ — with their audio bridged through a synthetic HF channel on the backend. No radio is keyed. ARDOP has a link layer, so lost blocks are retransmitted: at a workable SNR the image arrives intact, the modems adapt the rate down as conditions worsen, and below the cliff the connection never closes. ARDOP is a known-good external baseline — the bar Sonde is being built to meet — and is never reimplemented here.

What you're seeing

  1. Set the channel, then run a session. Drag SNR, pick a condition, choose the ARQ bandwidth ceiling (the widest mode allowed), and press Run connected session. A session is real airtime — give it time to play out.
  2. Two real ARDOP stations connect. The backend runs a real CONNECT handshake, negotiates a bandwidth, and transfers a ~3 KB image over adaptive-rate ARQ — their audio bridged through a synthetic HF channel. Real modulation, real FEC, real retransmission.
  3. Watch it adapt. The connection/ARQ log streams the handshake, the negotiated mode, and every rate change; the recon photo lands intact (ARQ retransmits) — slower as the rate drops, and not at all once the SNR falls below the connect cliff. Press play to hear the on-air audio and sweep its live spectrum.
